Where should "My Projects" live in the navigation?

Consider: how often will users switch between projects, and where does it feel natural?

A

TopBar button (always visible in explorer)

A small "Projects" button in the explorer's top bar, next to the existing Home and Profile buttons. Clicking it opens a dropdown or navigates to a project list page. Always one click away while exploring.

B

Replace the Upload screen as the main hub

The /upload page becomes a combined "dashboard" — your recent projects at the top, then the upload/GitHub URL input below. This is what you see after signing in. No new route needed.

C

Dedicated /projects route + nav link

A standalone page at /projects showing all your analyzed codebases as cards with name, framework, date, concept count. Linked from the landing page navbar, the upload screen, and the explorer top bar.

D

Option B + A combined (Recommended)

The upload screen becomes the hub (recent projects + upload below), AND there's a small "Projects" link in the explorer top bar to get back to it. Best of both: natural entry point + always accessible.
